Rachel Sklar

Rachel's passion for poop started in Nicaragua and has taken her to both field and laboratory settings, working as a research associate at UC San Francisco and managing sanitation projects in Nicaragua and Haiti. Rachel is also a lover of the arts and a practicing musician. She teaches a course at California College of the Arts on the role of science in studio art, part of the National Science Foundation's STEAM initiative, adding art and design components, the "A," to science, technology, engineering and mathematics (STEM) teaching and learning. A native Californian, she loves cooking, playing music, and seeing the world from a bicycle seat.

Giovanni Alvarado

Giovanni Alvarado was born and raised in Masaya, Nicaragua. Trained as a computer engineer, he started his work in public health when he was hired by PATH, a nonprofit organization that focuses on technical solutions that improve health in developing nations. Gio believes that his country's bright future starts with the health of the Nicaraguan people. Though he fights the spread of diseases, he can't help but infect others with his contagious smile and warm enthusiasm.

Mike Teez

A Business Administration and Ethnic Studies Double Major at Cal, Mike is also an EDM Producer / DJ / Record Label Owner creating a unique business model to bring philanthropy into the Dance music industry. He is passionate about sustainability and spends his extra-curriculars interning for La Peña Cultural Center in Berkeley, as well as putting together charity events for causes such as purchasing rainforest land in Brazil to avoid deforestation, buying school uniforms for children in Costa Rica, and distributing TOMS shoes amongst the homeless in Berkeley.

Megan Maurino

Megan Maurino is a student in the College of Natural Resources studying Conservation and Resource Studies with a concentration in Hydrological Engineering. She is currently working for the Physical Plant and Campus Services at UC Berkeley as a Water Conservation Intern. A wide variety of projects that she has been a part of include a campus wide energy conservation effort which documented how much energy/water heating systems are losing due to heat inefficiency. Additionally she is project leader of a pilot project that involves converting two buildings on the UCB campus to low-flow toilets. She has experience working with plumbing staff, stationary engineers, and the UCB Energy Office. Megan also shows an understanding and interest in sustainability by being the Sustainability Director of Amnesty International at Cal, part of a human rights organization for Cal students. When she is not working on designing water systems or advocating for the rights of her fellow community members, you will can catch Megan's radiant smile as she pirouettes to rhythms of contemporary jazz.

Staci Fox

Staci has a background in sociology and world development. She has worked passionately for organizations such as Kiva, Greenpeace, and FeelGood World. Working with Sani Salud and Give a Crap brings Staci much joy, as it combines two of her favorite things: art and social justice. She has also been a photographer for 15 years. Staci currently works at a middle school in Oakland as an administrator, photography teacher and basketball coach.

Jason Cristobal

Jason is SaniSalud's graphic designer and webmaster, and has been part of SaniSalud since the very beginning. Having been born in a country with frequent natural disasters (Philippines), he understands the importance of sanitation.
